Monarch Butterfly

\n

\n

(Female pictured) Similar looking to Viceroy)\nbutterflys, but can be distinguished by looking at the vein patterns. Notice here:

\n

\n

With the monarch on the left, and the viceroy on the right, one can tell the difference by looking\nat the vein parallel to the edge of the wing.

\n
    \n
  • https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monarch_butterfly
    • \n
  • https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viceroy_(butterfly))
    • \n
\n

Males can be identified from females by looking for a spot on one\nof the veins.

\n

| male | female\n| :--- | :----\n| |

\n

On the bottom wings of the male, there are black spots, but there are no spots on the female.
